# Privacy Policy

**Last Updated: July 8, 2026**

This Privacy Policy describes how QuickCard4AnkiDroid ("the App") collects, uses, and shares your information when you use our mobile application.

## 1. Information Collection and Use

The App is designed to be privacy-friendly. We do not require you to create an account or provide personal information to use the core features of the App. However, we use third-party services that may collect information used to identify you or your device.

### a. Automatically Collected Information
Through **Google Firebase (Analytics and Crashlytics)**, we automatically collect certain technical information:
*   **Device Information:** Device model, operating system version, and unique device identifiers (e.g., Firebase Installation ID).
*   **Usage Data:** How you interact with the App, including search terms (headwords) you look up, language preferences, and features you use.
*   **Crash Reports:** In the event of an error, we receive technical logs to help us improve stability.

### b. Information You Provide Voluntarily
*   **Feedback:** If you choose to send feedback through the App, you may voluntarily provide your **name** and **email address**. This information is stored in **Google Firebase (Firestore)** solely to respond to your inquiry and improve the App. We also collect device model and OS version with feedback to help troubleshoot issues.

## 2. Third-Party Services

We use Google Firebase to provide analytics, crash reporting, cloud messaging, and feedback storage. These services are provided by Google LLC and are subject to the [Google Privacy Policy](https://policies.google.com/privacy).

Specifically, the App uses:
*   **Firebase Analytics:** To understand how the app is used.
*   **Firebase Crashlytics:** To monitor and fix app crashes.
*   **Firebase Cloud Messaging:** To send notifications or updates (if enabled).
*   **Firebase Firestore:** To store user-submitted feedback.

## 3. Data Storage and Transfer

Your search history and Anki card data are stored locally on your device or within the AnkiDroid application. We do not have access to your Anki collection. Feedback and analytics data are stored on Google’s servers.

## 4. Children’s Privacy

The App does not address anyone under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personally identifiable information from children under 13.

## 5. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update our Privacy Policy from time to time. You are advised to review this page periodically for any changes.

## 6. Contact Us

If you have any questions or suggestions about this Privacy Policy, please contact us via the feedback form within the App or through the GitHub repository.
